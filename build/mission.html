Our Mission and Progress

We can minimise plastic pollution by reducing the plastic we use and recycling correctly.

Beach Cleaning Guide

  1. Gather gloves, bags and a litter picker
  2. Choose a local beach
  3. Encourage friends to join you
  4. Pick up any litter on the beach
  5. Dispose of your litter properly

Elliot's Editorial

Did You Know that our ocean currents can impact plastic pollution? Currents carry plastic from one place to another, sometimes taking it far away. When the currents meet, the plastic gathers together. When the currents slow down the plastic sinks to the sea bed or washes up on our beaches.

WasteShark

NI charity Citizen Sea use a remote-controlled water drone to retrieve floating rubbish from harbours and marinas. It was designed to mimic a basking shark with a large mouth to filter the water as it moves. It collects plastic and other debris then returns it to shore to be sorted and disposed of.

Using Less Plastic

  1. Bring your own shopping bags
  2. Carry a reusable water bottle
  3. Switch to paper straws
  4. Use a bamboo toothbrush
  5. Avoid plastic packaging
  6. Buy second hand items
  7. Use refillable products
